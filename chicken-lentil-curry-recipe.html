One-Pot Chicken & Red Lentil Curry

🛒 Ingredients (for 14)

🍗 Protein

  • 2 kg chicken breast or boneless thighs, diced (2–3 cm cubes)
  • 700g red lentils (dried, rinsed)

🧅 Aromatics & Veggies

  • 3 large onions, diced
  • 8 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large piece ginger (~5 cm), grated
  • 3 carrots, diced small
  • 300g baby spinach (or frozen spinach)
  • 2 cans (800g) crushed tomatoes

🌿 Spices & Flavour

  • 3 tbsp curry powder
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp paprika (sweet or smoked)
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt & black pepper

🥛 Liquid & Base

  • 2 cans (800ml) coconut milk (light or full-fat)
  • 1.5 L chicken stock (or water + bouillon cubes)
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • Fresh coriander for garnish
  • 1 kg basmati or jasmine rice (to serve)

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

1 Sear the Chicken 8–10 min
  • Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a very large pot (or do this in 2–3 batches in a regular pot) over high heat.
  • Season the diced chicken generously with salt, pepper, and 1 tbsp curry powder.
  • Sear the chicken in a single layer — don’t stir for 2–3 minutes so it gets a nice golden crust, then flip and sear the other side.
  • Remove chicken and set aside. It doesn’t need to be cooked through — it’ll finish in the curry.
💡 Tip: Don’t overcrowd the pot or the chicken will steam instead of sear. Work in batches for that golden colour — it adds a ton of flavour. If you’re short on time, you can skip the searing and add raw chicken at step 4 — it will still taste great.
2 Cook the Aromatics 5 min
  • In the same pot (with all the tasty chicken bits at the bottom), add 1 tbsp olive oil and reduce heat to medium.
  • Add the diced onions and cook for 3–4 minutes until softened and translucent.
  • Add the garlic and ginger and stir for 1 minute until fragrant.
  • Add the diced carrots and stir for another minute.
3 Toast the Spices 2 min
  • Add all the remaining spices: 2 tbsp curry powder, cumin, turmeric, paprika, garam masala, cayenne.
  • Stir everything together for 1–2 minutes — this blooms the spices and makes the whole kitchen smell amazing.
💡 Tip: If the pot looks dry, add a splash of stock to deglaze before adding spices. This prevents burning and lifts all the caramelized chicken fond from the bottom.
4 Add Lentils, Liquid & Simmer 25–30 min
  • Add the crushed tomatoes, coconut milk, and chicken stock. Stir well.
  • Add the rinsed red lentils and bring everything to a boil.
  • Reduce to a gentle simmer, cover with a lid (slightly cracked), and cook for 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • The lentils should be completely soft and breaking apart — this naturally thickens the curry into a creamy consistency.
  • Add the seared chicken back in during the last 10 minutes of simmering so it finishes cooking through without drying out.
💡 Tip: Red lentils cook fast and dissolve into the sauce — that’s exactly what you want! They create a thick, creamy, protein-rich base without any cream. If the curry gets too thick, add a splash of stock or water.
5 Add Spinach & Finish 3 min
  • Stir in the baby spinach in big handfuls — it wilts down in seconds.
  • Squeeze in the juice of 2 limes and stir.
  • Taste and adjust salt, pepper, and cayenne to your liking.
💡 Tip: The lime juice at the end is crucial — it brightens the whole dish and balances the richness of the coconut milk. Don’t skip it!
6 Cook the Rice 15–20 min (parallel)
  • While the curry simmers, cook 1 kg basmati or jasmine rice according to package instructions.
  • For 1 kg rice, use roughly 1.8 L water, a pinch of salt, bring to boil, then cover and cook on very low heat for 12–15 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
💡 Tip: Start the rice when the curry starts simmering (step 4) — they’ll be ready at the same time. For extra protein, you can swap half the rice for quinoa.
7 Serve! 🎉
  • Serve the curry over rice in big bowls.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander, an extra squeeze of lime, and optionally some plain Greek yogurt on top (even more protein!).
  • Set out naan bread or pita on the side if you want extra carbs for the hungry ones.

💪 Pro Tips for Feeding 14

🍳
Use the Biggest Pot You Have
A 10–12L stock pot is ideal. If you only have smaller pots, split into two batches — the recipe works exactly the same.
❄️
Freezes Perfectly
The curry (without rice) freezes beautifully for 2–3 months. Make extra and freeze portions for easy future meals.
💪
Even More Protein?
Add a can of drained chickpeas at step 4, or serve with a dollop of Greek yogurt (~10g extra protein per serving).
⏰
Make It Ahead
This curry tastes even better the next day as the flavours deepen. Make it the day before your event and simply reheat.
🌶️
Spice Level Control
Start with less cayenne and offer hot sauce on the side. This way everyone from kids to spice lovers is happy.
🌱
Make It Veggie
Skip the chicken entirely and double the lentils to 1.4 kg. Add a can of chickpeas and extra veggies (sweet potato, cauliflower). Still ~35g protein per serving.
