🚨 Chicken Safety — Read First

Raw chicken can carry bacteria. Follow these rules every single time:

  • Separate cutting boards: Use one cutting board for raw meat (ideally plastic — easier to sanitize) and another for everything else (vegetables, bread, etc.)
  • Wash hands & surfaces with hot soapy water immediately after touching raw chicken — before touching anything else
  • Never reuse utensils (knife, plate, fork) that touched raw chicken on cooked food without washing them first
  • Internal temperature: Chicken is safe at 74°C / 165°F — use a meat thermometer if you have one
  • Don't wash raw chicken under the tap — it splashes bacteria around your sink. Pat dry with paper towels instead

👨‍🍳 Steps

1 Preheat the oven

Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F) with fan/convection if available. Line a baking tray with parchment paper.

2 Butterfly the chicken breast 3 min

Place the chicken breast flat on your meat cutting board. With a sharp knife held parallel to the board, slice horizontally through the thickest part — don't cut all the way through. Open it like a book so the breast is an even thickness throughout (~1.5–2 cm).

💡 Why? Even thickness = even cooking. A thick breast will be dry on the outside and raw in the middle. This is the single most important step.
3 Dry the chicken 1 min

Pat the chicken breast thoroughly dry on both sides with paper towels. Throw the paper towels away immediately and wash your hands.

💡 Why dry? Moisture on the surface = steaming instead of browning. Dry chicken gets a better sear, better spice adhesion, and better texture.
4 Season with salt

Sprinkle salt generously on both sides of the chicken. Don't be shy — some will fall off during cooking.

5 Make the spice rub 2 min

Mix the spices of your chosen rub in a small bowl (see the spice selector above). If using the simple option, just have your pepper grinder ready.

6 Oil & rub the chicken 2 min

Drizzle a small amount of olive oil (~1 tsp per breast) directly onto the chicken. Rub the oil over the entire surface with your hands. Then generously apply the spice rub on both sides. Really press it in — use your hands and massage the spices into the meat.

⚠️ Reminder: Your hands have now touched raw chicken. Do NOT touch anything else (spice jars, olive oil bottle, oven handle) until you've washed your hands with soap. Prepare everything you need before touching the chicken.
7 Bake 20–25 min

Place the chicken on the prepared baking tray. Bake at 200°C for 20–25 minutes depending on thickness. The chicken is done when:

  • Internal temperature reaches 74°C / 165°F
  • Juices run clear when you cut into the thickest part (no pink)
  • The outside has a nice golden colour
8 Rest before cutting 5 min

Take the chicken out and let it rest on the tray for 5 minutes before slicing. This lets the juices redistribute — if you cut too early, all the moisture runs out and you get dry chicken.

💬 Tips & Good to Know

🚫
Don't overcook!
Chicken breast goes from juicy to dry in just 2–3 minutes. Set a timer. Better to check early than to guess.
🎯
Invest in a thermometer
A meat thermometer removes all guesswork. Pull at 72°C — carry-over heat will bring it to 74°C while resting.
🪥
Two cutting boards
Keep a plastic board for raw meat (dishwasher-safe, easy to sanitize) and a wooden/other board for everything else. This prevents cross-contamination.
🛢️
Oil = hidden calories
Even olive oil is ~120 kcal/tbsp. Use a teaspoon or a spray bottle. A light coat is all you need for the spices to stick.
❄️
Batch cook & freeze
Cook 4–6 breasts at once. Slice, portion into containers. Fridge (4 days) or freezer (3 months). Reheat in microwave or oven.
🥩
Don't skip the butterfly
An uneven breast cooks unevenly. The thin part dries out while the thick part is still raw. Always flatten for consistent results.
