🛒 Ingredients

🥣 Bowl

  • 1 banana (medium size)
  • 2 scoops Protéalpes vanilla isolate whey (gamme sans sucre)
  • 1 tablespoon dark cocoa powder (unsweetened)
  • 1 tablespoon oat milk (or any milk of your choice)
  • ~30g oats (rolled or instant)
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds (optional)

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

1 Prep the Ingredients 1 min
  • Peel the banana and set aside — you’ll crush it with a fork in the next step.
  • Measure out 2 scoops of Protéalpes vanilla isolate whey.
  • Measure 1 tablespoon of dark cocoa powder (unsweetened) and 1 tablespoon of chia seeds.
  • Have your 1 tablespoon of oat milk and ~30g oats ready.
💡 Tip: A just-yellow banana keeps the sweetness in check. Too ripe and the cocoa flavour gets lost. Any ripeness crushes fine with a fork.
2 Crush & Mix 1 min
  • Peel the banana into a bowl and crush it thoroughly with a fork until you get a smooth mash.
  • Add the oat milk (just 1 tablespoon) and stir with the fork to loosen the banana mash slightly.
  • Add the protein powder, cocoa powder, oats, and chia seeds.
  • Mix vigorously with the fork for 30–45 seconds until everything is well combined into a thick porridge.
  • If you can’t mix through the dry parts, add 1 more teaspoon of milk at a time. It should stay thick and spoonable.
💡 Tip: Crush the banana first before adding the powders — it’s much easier to get a smooth, lump-free mix this way.
3 Top & Enjoy 30 sec
  • Optionally top with a few rolled oats, chia seeds, banana slices, and a dust of cocoa powder for presentation.
  • Eat with a spoon — enjoy it fresh or cover and refrigerate overnight for a ready-to-eat breakfast.
  • That’s it — ~63g of protein in under 3 minutes. 💪
💡 Tip: Works great as overnight prep — chia seeds and oats absorb the liquid and thicken into a dense, pudding-like texture by morning. Just stir and eat cold, or warm it up briefly.

💡 Pro Tips

🍌
Banana Ripeness
A slightly green or just-yellow banana keeps the sweetness balanced — fully ripe bananas can make it overwhelmingly sweet. Less ripe ones are firmer but crush fine with a fork.
⏰
Post-Workout
Ideal within 30 minutes after training. The fast-digesting whey isolate + banana carbs = rapid muscle recovery.
🌍
Milk Alternatives
Oat milk works great, but almond milk (even fewer calories) or cow’s milk (more protein) are excellent alternatives.
🌿
Level Up
Add a tablespoon of peanut butter (+8g protein, +healthy fats) or a handful of spinach (invisible in taste, adds iron & folate).
