Smashed Burger in Pizza Dough

Crispy smashed steaks, caramelized toppings, wrapped in golden pizza dough — Pour 2 burgers

🍽️ Serving Portions

This recipe makes 2 wrapped burgers — 1 larger and 1 slightly smaller:

Burger Steak weight Size
🟢 Burger #1 225g Large
🟡 Burger #2 175g Regular

🛒 Ingredients

🥩 Burger Steaks

  • 400g steak haché 5% matière grasse
  • Olive oil (or neutral oil)
  • Salt & black pepper
  • Piment de Cayenne (optional)

🍕 Dough & Finish

  • Pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • Egg yolk (optional — for brushing)
  • Sesame seeds (optional)

🧅 Caramelized Toppings

  • Onion(s), sliced (optional)
  • Mushrooms, sliced (optional)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt & pepper
  • Herbes de Provence (optional)

🥒 Sauces & Extras

  • Mayonnaise (optional)
  • Mustard (optional)
  • Ketchup (optional)
  • Pickles, sliced (optional)
  • Dried tomatoes (optional)

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

1 Smash & Sear the Burger Steaks 8–10 min
  • Divide the 400g steak haché into two portions: 225g and 175g.
  • Shape each portion into a ball, then place on a very hot pan or skillet with a bit of olive oil (or neutral oil).
  • Immediately smash the ball flat using a spatula, heavy plate, or burger press — make them thin. This is the smashed burger technique!
  • Season the top with salt and pepper while the bottom sears.
  • Cook on high heat for about 2–3 minutes per side until you get a deep caramelized, crispy crust.
  • At the very end (last 30 seconds), sprinkle piment de Cayenne if desired.
  • Remove from pan and set aside.
💡 Tip: The key to a great smashed burger is maximum heat and not moving the patty while it sears. Don’t press it once it’s flat — let the Maillard reaction do its thing! The high heat + thin patty = incredible caramelization and crispiness.
2 Caramelize the Onions & Mushrooms 10–15 min
  • In the same pan (or a fresh one), add a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat.
  • Add sliced onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until golden and caramelized (~10 min). Season with salt, pepper, and optionally herbes de Provence.
  • In a separate area of the pan (or a second pan), sauté sliced mushrooms until golden brown and all moisture has evaporated. Season the same way.
  • Set both aside.
💡 Tip: Don’t rush the caramelization! Low-and-slow gives the sweetest, most flavourful onions. You can start the onions while the steaks are resting after cooking.
3 Preheat the Oven 1 min
  • Preheat your oven to 200–220°C (fan-assisted recommended).
  • Line a baking tray with parchment paper.
4 Assemble the Burger in Pizza Dough 5–8 min

This is the fun part! Repeat for each burger:

  • Take your pizza dough and divide it in two (proportional to each burger size).
  • Roll/stretch each piece into a thin, round layer on a lightly floured surface — large enough to completely wrap around the filling.
  • Layer 1 — Sauce: In the center of the dough circle, spread your sauces (mayo, mustard, ketchup — don’t overdo it).
  • Layer 2 — Steak: Place the smashed burger steak on top of the sauce.
  • Layer 3 — Toppings: Add caramelized onions, mushrooms, pickles, dried tomatoes — whatever you like. Don’t go too crazy with the quantity — just enough to complement, not overflow.
  • Fold & Seal: Carefully lift the edges of the dough up and over the filling, folding them toward the center on top. Pinch the dough together firmly to seal. Smooth out the top with your hands to form a neat dome/bun shape.
  • Place directly on the lined baking tray (seam-side up) — no need to flip! The egg wash and sesame seeds will go right on top of the sealed seam.
⚠️ Important: Keep the fillings in the center of the dough and leave enough border (at least 5–6 cm) around the edges to fold over. If the dough tears while folding, just pinch it back together — it will seal in the oven.

5 Egg Wash & Sesame Seeds 2 min
  • Beat an egg yolk with a tiny splash of water.
  • Using a brush (or your fingers), gently paint the sealed top of each wrapped burger with the egg yolk mixture. This gives a beautiful golden, glossy finish when baked and helps hide the seam.
  • Sprinkle sesame seeds on top — they’ll toast in the oven and give a classic burger bun look!
💡 Tip: The egg wash is purely cosmetic (and delicious). If you skip it, the dough will still bake fine — just a bit more matte in appearance.
6 Bake! 15–20 min
  • Place the tray in the preheated oven at 200–220°C.
  • Bake for 15–20 minutes, or until the dough is golden brown all over.
  • Let them rest for 2–3 minutes before cutting or serving.
💡 Tip: Every oven is different — keep an eye on them after 12 minutes. The dough should be golden, firm to the touch, and sound hollow when you tap the top.
7 Serve & Enjoy! 🎉
  • Cut in half to reveal the beautiful layers inside, or serve whole.
  • Pair with a side salad, fries, or enjoy on its own!

💪 Pro Tips

🔥
Smash Technique
Use a spatula or heavy flat surface. Smash once, hard, within the first 30 seconds of placing on the pan. Then leave it alone! The thinner the patty, the crispier the crust.
🧅
Don’t Overfill
It’s tempting to load up the fillings, but restraint is key. Too much filling makes folding difficult and can cause the dough to break or leak in the oven.
🎯
Seal It Well
Pinch the dough edges together firmly at the top. The seam stays on top — no need to flip! The egg wash and sesame seeds will cover and seal the seam beautifully in the oven.
🌮
Thin Dough = Better Result
Roll the dough as thin as you comfortably can. Thick dough will result in a bready texture that overwhelms the filling. Thin dough bakes crisp and golden.
