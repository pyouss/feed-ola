🛒 Ingredients

🐔 Oven-Baked Chicken

  • 750g chicken breast filets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Piment de Cayenne (pinch)
  • Herbes de Provence

🍝 Pasta

  • 350g spaghetti complet (whole wheat)
  • Water + salt for boiling
  • Reserve 200ml pasta water!

🥦 Vegetables & Sauce

  • 1 courgette (zucchini)
  • 2 onions
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • ~400ml tomato coulis (passata)
  • Salt & black pepper
  • Herbes de Provence
  • Piment de Cayenne (pinch)

🥗 Side Salad

  • Fresh baby spinach (~100g)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Juice of ½ lemon or a drizzle of vinaigre balsamique
  • Pinch of salt

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

1 Prepare & Season the Chicken 10 min
  • Preheat your oven to 220°C (fan-assisted) / 230°C (conventional).
  • Take the chicken breast filets and cut each one in half horizontally (butterfly style) to make them thinner — about 1 cm thick. This ensures even, fast cooking.
  • Place the chicken pieces on a lined baking tray (parchment paper or aluminium foil) in a single layer, not overlapping.
  • Drizzle 2 tablespoons of olive oil over the chicken.
  • Season generously with salt, black pepper, a pinch of piment de Cayenne, and herbes de Provence.
  • Rub the seasoning into each piece with your hands, coating both sides evenly.
💡 Tip: Let the chicken sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before baking — it cooks more evenly.
2 Bake the Chicken 18-20 min
  • Place the baking tray in the preheated oven at 220°C.
  • Bake for 18–20 minutes until the chicken is golden brown on the edges and cooked through (internal temperature should reach 74°C / 165°F).
  • Remove from the oven and tent loosely with aluminium foil.
  • Let it rest for 5 minutes — this keeps the juices in and makes slicing easier.
  • After resting, cut the chicken into small cubes (~1 cm). Set aside.
💡 Tip: Don't skip the resting step! Cutting immediately releases all the juices and makes the chicken dry.
3 Prep & Cook the Vegetables ~15 min

Start this while the chicken is in the oven — timing works out perfectly!

  • Dice the courgette into small cubes (~1 cm).
  • Slice the onions thinly (émincé).
  • Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large casserole / deep pan over medium heat.
  • Add the sliced onions and cook for ~5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until translucent and soft.
  • Add the diced courgettes, season with salt and pepper.
  • Cook for ~8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the courgettes are tender and lightly golden.
💡 Tip: Don't salt the courgettes too early or they'll release water and steam instead of getting a nice sear. Add salt once they are in the pan and starting to cook.
4 Cook the Pasta (Simultaneously!) 10-12 min
  • At the same time as the vegetables, bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil.
  • Add plenty of salt (about 1 tablespoon per litre of water) — the water should taste like the sea.
  • Add the 350g spaghetti complet and cook for 10–12 minutes (check package instructions) until al dente — firm to the bite.
  • ⚠️ IMPORTANT: Before draining, reserve about 200ml (a small mug) of pasta water. This starchy water is liquid gold for your sauce!
  • Drain the pasta and set aside.
💡 Tip: The pasta water is salty! Keep this in mind when seasoning the tomato sauce in the next step — go lighter on salt in the sauce.
5 Make the Tomato Sauce 5 min
  • Once the courgettes and onions are cooked, pour in the ~400ml tomato coulis (adjust to your preference — more for saucier, less for drier).
  • Season with salt, pepper, herbes de Provence, and a pinch of piment de Cayenne.
  • Stir well and let it simmer for about 5 minutes on medium-low heat to let the flavours come together.
  • Taste and adjust! Remember: the pasta water you'll add is salty, so keep the sauce slightly under-seasoned at this stage.
⚠️ Important: Go easy on the salt here! The pasta water is salty, and once you add it the seasoning will intensify. You can always add more salt at the end.
6 Combine Everything! 3 min
  • Add the drained spaghetti directly into the casserole with the tomato sauce and vegetables.
  • Pour in 50–100ml of the reserved pasta water — this helps the sauce coat every strand of pasta and gives a silky texture.
  • Toss everything together over low heat for 1–2 minutes.
  • Add the cubed chicken and gently fold in so the chicken is evenly distributed.
  • Taste and adjust seasoning one final time — add salt, pepper, or piment de Cayenne as needed.
💡 Tip: If the pasta looks too dry, add a splash more pasta water. If too saucy, let it simmer for another minute. The starch in the pasta water helps everything bind together beautifully.
7 Prepare the Side Salad 2 min
  • Place fresh baby spinach in a bowl.
  • Drizzle with 1 tablespoon olive oil and the juice of half a lemon or a drizzle of vinaigre balsamique — both work great!
  • Add a pinch of salt and toss gently.
  • Serve alongside the pasta for a fresh, light contrast and extra nutrients.
💡 Tip: You can also add cherry tomatoes, cucumber, or a sprinkle of seeds to the salad for extra crunch and nutrition.

⭐ Pro Tips

⏱️
Timing Coordination
Start the vegetables when the chicken goes in the oven. Put the pasta on to boil 5 minutes later. Everything finishes around the same time!
💧
Pasta Water is Gold
The starchy, salty water helps sauce cling to pasta and creates a silky texture. Always save some before draining!
🐔
Rest Your Chicken
5 minutes of resting = juicy chicken. Skipping this step means dry, tough cubes. Patience pays off!
🧂
Salt in Layers
Season at every step (chicken, pasta water, vegetables, sauce) but go lighter each time. You can always add more at the end — you can't take it away!
🔥
Cayenne Control
Piment de Cayenne is potent — start with a small pinch and build up. You can always add more heat, but you can't remove it!
💪
Boost Protein Further
Swap regular spaghetti for protein pasta (chickpea or lentil-based) to add 10–15g extra protein per serving.
